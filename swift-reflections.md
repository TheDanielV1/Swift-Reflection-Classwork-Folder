### What Makes E-Lit

To me, a piece of E-Literature distinct from what is consdiered regular literature is the fact that E-lit is almost a hybrid between regular lit and various forms of digital media. what it is definitley not is a text which was intially a printed work that was transitioned onto a digital format, such as e-books on kidnle, and also should not be easy to transform from being on a digital device into a printed physical copy. the unqiueness of E-lit is its variety, and the fact that the reading of the text becomes an active expereince. this can be as simple such as hovering over certain words to reveal for infromation, transistioning to different pages through hyperlinks. Or it can become more complex, like an interactive story where you are essentially co-authroing the text, an example of this being the 'first draft of the revolution' by and shade. out of the examples provide during the class it is hard for me to say which i enjoyed most. While i loved the activeness of shade which had you in a at first gance mundane setting which is transformed through the reader inputting commands for the character to progress the story, I found the activeness was more of the focual point at has diminished my ability to actually absorb the story being told. I beleive I liked the gathering cloud the most, as it was a more standard piece of writing that could be followed, with the hyperlinks and extra information found through interacting with the piece adding to the depth of what I was reading. Through this course I expect to learn how to operate in different coding types in order to create a unique story which is further enhanced by the transformative meeting that is E-Literature.
